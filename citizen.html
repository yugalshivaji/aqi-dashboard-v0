Complaint Guidelines

Pollution Health Symptoms

Seek Medical Help If You Experience:
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or tightness
  • Severe coughing or wheezing
  • Eye irritation or redness
  • Nausea or dizziness
  • Worsening of asthma or COPD symptoms
Tip: Keep your medications handy during high pollution days

AQI Shelters

Find clean air shelters during high pollution episodes

Available Shelters

AQI Shelters provide clean air environments during severe pollution episodes (AQI > 300). They are equipped with air purifiers, medical assistance, and basic amenities.

Shelter Guidelines

When to Use AQI Shelters:
  • AQI exceeds 300 (Very Poor to Severe)
  • You experience breathing difficulties
  • You belong to sensitive groups (children, elderly, pregnant women)
  • You have respiratory conditions (asthma, COPD)
  • During pollution emergencies announced by government
What to Bring:
  • Identification proof (Aadhar Card, Voter ID)
  • Personal medications
  • Face masks (if available)
  • Water bottle
  • Essential personal items

Diet Tips
Foods for Lung Health:
  • Turmeric (anti-inflammatory)
  • Ginger (clears respiratory tract)
  • Honey (soothes throat)
  • Green leafy vegetables
  • Citrus fruits (Vitamin C)
Indoor Tips
Improve Indoor Air:
  • Use air purifiers
  • Keep windows closed during high AQI
  • Regular cleaning to reduce dust
  • Indoor plants (Areca palm, Spider plant)
  • Use exhaust fans in kitchen
Outdoor Tips
When Going Out:
  • Check AQI before going out
  • Wear N95 masks when AQI > 200
  • Avoid peak pollution hours (morning/evening)
  • Choose less polluted routes
  • Carry inhaler if asthmatic
